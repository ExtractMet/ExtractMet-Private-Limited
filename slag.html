Slag Valorisation & Circular Economy

Transforming furnace slag and mining waste from a disposal liability into a revenue-generating resource—through chemistry characterisation, process design, and market-linked application development.

Applications

Every tonne of slag has hidden value

Steel and ferroalloy plants generate hundreds of thousands of tonnes of slag annually. With the right characterisation and treatment, slag becomes a marketable product—reducing disposal costs, generating revenue, and contributing to ESG goals.

Cement & Construction Materials

BF slag ground granulated (GGBS) as a supplementary cementitious material—replacing up to 70% of Portland clinker. Reduces embodied carbon and production cost per tonne of concrete.

Geopolymer Binders

Alkali-activated slag geopolymers as zero-cement binders with superior chemical resistance—for precast, infrastructure, and underground mining applications.

Critical Mineral Recovery

Hydrometallurgical and pyrometallurgical processing of slag to recover chromium, vanadium, manganese, and rare earth elements—converting disposal cost to commodity revenue.

Road Base & Aggregate

Processed EAF and BOF slag as high-strength road base aggregate—meeting IS and IRC standards. Dense, angular particles provide superior bearing capacity versus natural aggregate.

Agricultural Soil Amendment

BF slag as a liming agent to neutralise acidic soils and supply micronutrients (Ca, Mg, Si)—an emerging agromineral market particularly relevant in South and Southeast Asia.

Magnetising Roasting

Beneficiation of lean iron ores and mining waste via magnetising reduction roasting—converting non-magnetic iron minerals to magnetite for efficient magnetic separation and recycle.

The circular economy case for slag

Indian steel plants generate over 20 million tonnes of slag annually. The utilisation rate of BF slag for GGBS in India is below 50%—far below the 90%+ seen in Japan and South Korea. This gap represents a significant untapped revenue stream for forward-thinking producers.

ExtractMet provides end-to-end characterisation, process flow sheet development, and market linkage support to convert this liability into a competitive advantage.

  • XRD, SEM-EDS, XRF characterisation of slag mineralogy
  • Reactivity index and hydraulic activity testing (IS 455)
  • Custom process design for grinding, separation, and packaging
  • Market and off-take analysis for cement and infrastructure sectors
  • Carbon credit benefit quantification from landfill avoidance
