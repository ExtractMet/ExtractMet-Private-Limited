Expertise

SAF process intelligence for ferroalloy producers

Ferroalloy production—particularly ferrochrome (FeCr)—is energy-intensive, with electric energy costs comprising 30–50% of operating expenditure. Small improvements in energy efficiency and metal recovery translate directly to significant EBITDA gains.

ExtractMet applies fundamental thermodynamic models, combined with rigorous analysis of plant operating data, to identify and implement these improvements without costly equipment changes.

  • Submerged arc furnace (SAF) charge mix optimisation
  • Temperature profile and electrode management
  • Slag chemistry optimisation for maximum Cr recovery
  • Specific energy consumption reduction
  • Techno-economic assessment & environmental sustainability

What drives ferroalloy profitability?

In FeCr production, a 1% improvement in chromium recovery at a 100 kt/yr plant typically generates USD 1–2M in additional annual revenue. Combined with 5–8% energy savings—achievable through electrode positioning and charge mix optimisation—the financial case for optimisation is compelling.

ExtractMet's approach combines fundamental SAF thermodynamics with machine-learning analysis of your historical plant data to identify improvements invisible to conventional audits.

  • Typical Cr recovery improvement: 2–5 percentage points
  • Specific energy reduction: 5–10%
  • Slag basicity optimisation for improved metal-slag separation
  • Electrode immersion depth modelling for uniform burden heating
